PP01

Preparing Collingwood for the Future of Municipal Water Services

Keeping strategic options open for essential public infrastructure.

  1. Home
  2. Vision for Collingwood
  3. PP01

Why This Matters

Clean, safe and reliable drinking water is one of the most important services any municipality provides. Most residents rarely think about water and wastewater systems until something goes wrong. Responsible leadership means planning ahead so future generations continue to have dependable services, affordable rates and infrastructure capable of supporting sustainable growth.

Our Vision

Collingwood should protect and strengthen its publicly accountable water and wastewater services while remaining open to future governance and financing options that may help the community meet long-term infrastructure needs.

Guiding Principle: Keeping our options open is not indecision — it is prudent governance when long-term public infrastructure and millions of taxpayer dollars are involved.

1. Start From Strength

Collingwood has significant expertise, existing partnerships and an operating model that staff have described as strong. There is no need to pursue change simply for the sake of change.

2. Respect the Staff Report

The June 15, 2026 staff report identified key questions about governance, transparency, board composition, municipal influence and financial impacts.

3. Keep the Door Open

Council voted not to submit an expression of interest at that time. That decision should not become a decision to stop asking important questions.

Source context: Town of Collingwood Council/Committee of the Whole agenda, June 15, 2026, Staff Report CAO2026-05; CollingwoodToday reporting, June 19, 2026.

PP01

Understanding the Issue

What has changed — and what still needs to be answered

Understanding the Issue

The Context

The Province has opened discussion about new water and wastewater governance models, including public corporations and municipal services corporations. Regional conversations are also occurring because many municipalities face major infrastructure costs at the same time growth targets are increasing.

The Local Decision

On June 15, 2026, Collingwood Council received Staff Report CAO2026-05 on Municipal Servicing Corporations and Public Service Corporations. Council then voted not to submit an expression of interest at that time and to continue with the Town’s existing direction, including collaboration with other municipalities.

The Policy Question

The question is not whether Collingwood should create a corporation today. The better question is whether Collingwood should continue to seek answers so future Councils can make fully informed decisions if circumstances change.

Decision Framework

  1. Staff Report CAO2026-05 Technical foundation and unanswered questions identified
  2. Council Vote No expression of interest submitted at that time
  3. Outstanding Questions Governance, transparency, control, financing, asset transfer, liability
  4. Ongoing Monitoring Provincial policy, neighbouring municipal actions, County/regional discussions
  5. Future Decision Point If circumstances change, decide with evidence — not assumptions
  6. Public Accountability Maintain municipal transparency and resident confidence
  7. Long-Term Result Reliable, affordable, accountable services for future generations

Municipality’s Role

Town of Collingwood

Owns/operates local water and wastewater services; sets rates and plans local infrastructure.

Simcoe County / Region

May participate in regional infrastructure planning and coordination where municipalities agree.

Province of Ontario

Sets legislation, regulations and may create or enable new governance models; provides funding programs.

Federal Government

Supports major infrastructure through funding programs and national policy priorities.

Sources: Staff Report CAO2026-05, June 15, 2026; CollingwoodToday, June 19, 2026; OrilliaMatters, June 10, 2026.

PP01

The Plan

Stay informed, protect accountability and make evidence-based decisions

The Plan

Acknowledge the Work Already Done

Use the June 15, 2026 staff report as the starting point. Do not duplicate staff work or spend taxpayer dollars restudying the same questions.

Answer the Outstanding Questions

Before any future decision is made, obtain clear answers on governance, transparency, control, board composition, asset transfers, debt, rates and liabilities.

Monitor Provincial Direction

Track provincial legislation, funding programs, expressions of interest and any regional proposals that may affect Collingwood.

Protect Public Accountability

Any model considered in the future must preserve transparency, public oversight, reliable service and resident confidence.

Evaluate Financial Impacts

Compare lifecycle costs, borrowing limits, development charge impacts, rate implications and long-term taxpayer exposure.

Support Regional Cooperation

Continue practical collaboration with neighbouring municipalities where it strengthens service reliability, cost-sharing or regional planning.

Decision Test for Any Future Governance Option

Service

Will reliability improve?

Control

Will the Town retain adequate influence?

Cost

Are ratepayer and taxpayer impacts clear?

Transparency

Will decisions remain public and accountable?

Growth

Will it support sustainable growth?

What This Means for Residents

  • The Town should not rush into a new governance model.
  • The Town should not permanently close the door before key questions are answered.
  • Residents deserve clear information about service reliability, rates, debt, accountability and future growth.
  • Council should be prepared if provincial or regional decisions change the landscape.

Policy Position

Collingwood should continue operating from a position of strength while maintaining the knowledge, flexibility and public accountability needed to respond to future opportunities or risks. The goal is not structural change for its own sake; the goal is informed decision-making for essential public infrastructure.

PP01

What Success Looks Like

Reliable service, informed decisions and future-ready infrastructure

What Success Looks Like

Measuring Success

  • Reliable water quality and wastewater treatment performance
  • Full regulatory compliance and effective risk management
  • Transparent reporting to Council and the public
  • Clear understanding of long-term capital and rate impacts
  • Practical cooperation with neighbouring municipalities
  • Future governance options evaluated only with complete information

First 100 Days

Receive a briefing from staff on provincial developments, neighbouring municipal initiatives and responses to the unanswered questions identified in the June 15, 2026 staff report. Ensure Council maintains an informed understanding of emerging governance and financing models so future decisions are based on evidence rather than assumptions.

Deliverable: A public Council update identifying what is known, what remains unanswered, and what information should be monitored before any future decision is made.

Closing Message

The decisions Collingwood makes about water and wastewater services will shape the community for decades. By respecting the work already completed, asking the right questions and keeping future options open, Council can protect one of our most essential public services while preparing responsibly for growth.

This is not about rushing toward a Municipal Services Corporation. It is about ensuring Collingwood never loses an opportunity because it stopped paying attention too soon.

— Norm
