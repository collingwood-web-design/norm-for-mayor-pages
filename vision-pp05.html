PP05

Connecting Collingwood

Transportation, active transportation and accessibility — safe streets, complete communities, better connections.

Previous PP04 Financial Sustainability ALL PAPERS

Why This Matters

Transportation is about much more than roads. It is about how people of all ages and abilities move safely, efficiently and comfortably throughout Collingwood. Whether travelling by car, on foot, by bicycle, by transit or using mobility devices, residents depend upon an integrated transportation system that supports public safety, economic vitality and quality of life.

Our Vision

Collingwood should pursue a complete mobility strategy that safely and efficiently moves people — not simply vehicles. Decisions should balance the needs of motorists, pedestrians, cyclists, transit users, seniors, families, visitors and residents living with disabilities while protecting the character of our community.

Guiding Principle: Safe streets. Complete communities. Better connections. Build on the Town’s Transportation Master Plan and Active Transportation Plan — implement them, update them, and use them as the foundation for future decisions.

01

Build on Existing Plans

Implement and periodically update the Transportation Master Plan and Active Transportation Plan. Council should set priorities, remove barriers to delivery and keep transportation aligned with growth and community needs.

02

Safer Streets

Traffic safety remains one of the concerns raised most often by residents. Continue evidence-based traffic calming, improved crossings, school-zone safety and intersection improvements supported by engineering analysis and public consultation.

03

Active Transportation

Guide sidewalks, cycling routes, trails and connections between neighbourhoods, schools, parks, the waterfront, downtown and the Georgian Trail — improving health, reducing congestion and strengthening community life.

Understanding the Issue

How Collingwood can connect people of all ages and abilities across the community

Understanding the Issue

Accessibility

Universal accessibility should be incorporated into every transportation project from the outset. Future improvements should continue to be developed in consultation with the Accessibility Advisory Committee.

Downtown Mobility & Parking

Integrate downtown mobility with the broader strategy, including structured parking and better use of municipal lands explored in Publication No. 3. Continue improving management and efficiency of municipal parking assets where justified by evidence and community need.

Transit & Housing Connections

Public transit provides essential mobility for seniors, students, workers and residents who choose not to drive. Transportation and housing must be planned together so new neighbourhoods connect to employment, schools, shopping, recreation and transit.

Getting Started

  1. Review Priorities Identify implementation priorities in the Transportation Master Plan and Active Transportation Plan.
  2. Quick-Win Safety Advance practical safety improvements with clear engineering support.
  3. Accessibility Review Confirm accessibility priorities with the Accessibility Advisory Committee.
  4. County Dialogue Begin discussions with Simcoe County on shared transportation objectives.
  5. Build on Plans Use existing plans rather than commissioning unnecessary new studies.
  6. Align with Growth Keep mobility planning coordinated with housing and downtown initiatives.
  7. Report Progress Measure delivery so residents can see safer streets and better connections.

Shared Transportation Responsibilities

Town of Collingwood

Leads local roads, sidewalks, cycling connections, parking assets and implementation of Town transportation plans.

Simcoe County

Shares responsibility for County roads and regional transportation. The Mayor should advocate for Collingwood’s interests on County Council.

Province of Ontario

Sets highway and provincial transportation policy that shapes regional access and emergency response corridors.

Accessibility Partners

The Accessibility Advisory Committee and community partners help ensure universal design is built into every project.

Primary references include the Town of Collingwood Transportation Master Plan, Active Transportation Plan, Official Plan, Asset Management Plan and relevant Simcoe County transportation policies.

PP05

The Plan

Safer streets, better choices and complete connections for every resident

The Plan

01

Implement the Master Plans

Use the Transportation Master Plan and Active Transportation Plan as the foundation for priorities, capital decisions and ongoing updates.

02

Deliver Safety Improvements

Advance traffic calming, crossings, school-zone measures and intersection upgrades supported by evidence and consultation.

03

Expand Active Connections

Close gaps in sidewalks, cycling routes and trails linking neighbourhoods, schools, parks, downtown, the waterfront and the Georgian Trail.

04

Design for Accessibility

Build universal accessibility into every project from the start, in consultation with the Accessibility Advisory Committee.

05

Strengthen Downtown Mobility

Integrate parking management and downtown access into a complete mobility strategy, coordinated with Publication No. 3 initiatives.

06

Partner Regionally

Work with Simcoe County and explore practical transit connections so Collingwood residents gain safer, more reliable regional mobility.

Decision Test for Mobility Investments

Safety

Will it make streets safer for people of all ages and abilities?

Choice

Does it give people practical choices beyond driving alone?

Access

Is universal accessibility built in from the start?

Connection

Does it link neighbourhoods to jobs, schools, parks, downtown and transit?

Plan Fit

Does it advance priorities already set in the Town’s mobility plans?

What This Means for Residents

  • Streets should feel safer for pedestrians, cyclists, motorists and mobility-device users.
  • Neighbourhoods should connect more clearly to schools, parks, downtown and the waterfront.
  • Accessibility should be built into projects from day one, not added as an afterthought.
  • Existing plans should guide action — without spending on unnecessary new studies.

Policy Position

Collingwood should pursue a complete mobility strategy that moves people safely and efficiently, implements the Transportation Master Plan and Active Transportation Plan, improves accessibility and downtown connections, and works with Simcoe County where shared roads and regional mobility require a stronger local voice.

What Success Looks Like

Safer streets, stronger connections and plans that get delivered

What Success Looks Like

Measuring Success

  • Implementation of Transportation Master Plan priorities
  • Progress on the Active Transportation Plan
  • Safer streets and improved crossings
  • Stronger accessibility outcomes
  • Better downtown mobility and parking management
  • Clear collaboration with Simcoe County and higher resident satisfaction

First 100 Days / Getting Started

Review implementation priorities within the Transportation Master Plan and Active Transportation Plan, identify quick-win safety improvements, review accessibility priorities and begin discussions with Simcoe County on shared transportation objectives.

Deliverable: A public implementation briefing listing near-term safety projects, accessibility priorities and County partnership items for Council follow-up.

Closing Message

Connecting Collingwood means designing streets and networks for people of all ages and abilities. By implementing the plans we already have, improving safety and accessibility, strengthening downtown and neighbourhood connections and working regionally where it matters, Collingwood can deliver complete communities with better ways to get around.

— Norm
