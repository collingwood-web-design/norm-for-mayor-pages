My Priorities

Practical solutions for Collingwood Ideas, leadership, and results that help our community plan, build, and grow responsibly.

Housing Infrastructure Downtown Fiscal Leadership

Housing That Works for Local Residents

Supporting attainable housing solutions for working families, young professionals, seniors, and local employees while protecting the character of established neighbourhoods.

  • Attainable options for families, professionals, and seniors
  • Housing that works for people employed in Collingwood
  • Growth that respects established neighbourhoods

Infrastructure Before Crisis

Ensuring roads, water, wastewater, recreation facilities, and municipal services keep pace with growth through responsible long-term planning.

  • Plan ahead so core services are not left behind
  • Invest in roads, water, wastewater, and recreation
  • Manage major projects with municipal expertise

A Stronger Downtown

Addressing parking shortages, supporting local businesses, improving accessibility, and enhancing the vitality of Collingwood's historic downtown.

  • Practical answers to downtown parking pressures
  • Support for local businesses and main-street vitality
  • Improved accessibility in our historic core

Fiscal Responsibility and Accountability

Protecting taxpayers through disciplined budgeting, transparent decision-making, and a focus on municipal priorities.

  • Disciplined budgets focused on what matters most
  • Transparent decisions residents can trust
  • Spending taxpayer dollars wisely

Collaborative Leadership

Building productive working relationships with residents, businesses, neighbouring municipalities, provincial agencies, and community organizations.

  • Listen to residents and work with local businesses
  • Partner with neighbouring municipalities and Queen's Park
  • Bring people together to solve difficult issues

From Norm

Solutions, Not Personalities

Sandberg emphasized that his campaign will focus on solutions rather than personalities.

"Collingwood benefits when elections are about ideas, leadership, and results," he said. "I respect anyone willing to put their name forward for public service. Voters deserve a positive campaign focused on who is best prepared to lead our community through the opportunities and challenges ahead."

Sandberg noted that while many candidates can identify problems, his experience demonstrates an ability to deliver outcomes.

"My record includes helping build community facilities, advancing affordable housing, securing municipal interests at Queen's Park, managing major infrastructure projects, and bringing people together to solve difficult issues. I am running because I believe that experience, combined with a clear vision for the future, can help Collingwood achieve its full potential."

"Collingwood deserves leadership that understands how municipalities work, how infrastructure is funded, how housing projects are delivered, and how taxpayer dollars can be spent wisely. I am ready to bring that experience to the Mayor's office."
