PP07

Housing for a Growing Collingwood

Ensuring those who work here can build their future here.

Why This Matters

Housing is more than a roof over our heads. It creates opportunity, supports families, strengthens employers and helps build complete communities.

The Opportunity to Build a Future

Two years after Jan and I were married, we were expecting our first child while I was attending Georgian College. Pretty River Villas Co-operative Housing gave our young family the opportunity to establish roots, raise our children and build our future in Collingwood. Housing is not simply about buildings. It is about giving families the opportunity to build their future.

Guiding Principle: Those who work in Collingwood should have a realistic opportunity to live here and build their future here.

01

Build on Collingwood’s Tradition

Victory Village, innovative multiplex housing, the High Street initiative, the Affordable Housing Task Force and the Affordable Housing Master Plan provide a foundation for the next Council.

02

Support the Housing Continuum

Different solutions are needed for emergency shelter, supportive housing, workforce housing, rental housing, home ownership and seniors’ housing.

03

Build Community Support

Housing succeeds when residents understand why it is needed, how it fits into neighbourhoods and how it benefits the community.

Vision for Collingwood — Publication No. 7: Housing for a Growing Collingwood.

Understanding the Housing Challenge

Opportunity, infrastructure and partnerships across the housing continuum

Understanding the Housing Challenge

Those Who Work Here Should Be Able to Live Here

Teachers, nurses, tradespeople, first responders, municipal employees, hospitality workers, entrepreneurs and young families should have realistic opportunities to build their future in the community they serve.

Infrastructure Enables Housing

Housing depends on water, wastewater, transportation and other enabling infrastructure. Growth must be supported responsibly, consistent with PP01, PP04 and PP05.

Build on Recent Progress

The recently announced CHIPP program and “What’s Possible for Collingwood” campaign appear promising and deserve careful evaluation as they move from announcement to implementation. Programs should be judged by results.

The Town’s Role

  1. Provide Certainty Make planning expectations clear and predictable.
  2. Enable Growth Invest responsibly in water, wastewater and transportation.
  3. Support Partnerships Work with governments, builders and community organizations.
  4. Coordinate Regionally Collaborate with Simcoe County on shared housing responsibilities.
  5. Measure Results Evaluate programs by their real housing outcomes.

Housing Partners

Town of Collingwood

Provides planning certainty and invests in enabling local infrastructure.

Simcoe County

Leads important affordable housing, supportive housing and homelessness programs.

Senior Governments

Provide policy tools and funding needed to support housing and infrastructure.

Builders & Community

Deliver homes, partnerships and local support across the housing continuum.

Primary references include the Affordable Housing Task Force Report, Affordable Housing Master Plan, Official Plan, County housing strategy, Planning Act and Municipal Act, 2001.

The Plan

More options, enabling infrastructure and measurable housing outcomes

The Plan

01

Provide Planning Certainty

Give residents, builders and partners clear expectations about where and how appropriate housing can proceed.

02

Invest in Enabling Infrastructure

Coordinate housing growth with responsible water, wastewater, transportation and other infrastructure planning.

03

Convene a Housing Summit

Bring partners together in an implementation-focused summit to establish responsibilities, timelines and measurable outcomes.

04

Strengthen Partnerships

Work collaboratively with Simcoe County, senior governments, builders and community organizations.

05

Build Community Understanding

Explain why housing is needed, how it fits into neighbourhoods and the benefits it brings to the community.

06

Evaluate Programs by Results

Track whether initiatives move from announcements to implementation and deliver real housing outcomes.

Decision Test for Housing Initiatives

Opportunity

Will it help more people build a secure future in Collingwood?

Housing Choice

Does it add useful options across the housing continuum?

Infrastructure

Can water, wastewater and transportation support it responsibly?

Community Fit

Is the need, neighbourhood fit and community benefit clearly explained?

Results

Are responsibilities, timelines and measurable outcomes defined?

What This Means for Residents

  • Workers and young families should have more realistic opportunities to live locally.
  • Housing solutions should address needs across the full continuum.
  • Growth should proceed with responsible infrastructure planning.
  • Programs should be measured by homes and opportunities delivered.

Policy Position

The Town should provide planning certainty, invest in enabling infrastructure, support partnerships and work collaboratively with Simcoe County, senior governments, builders and community organizations.

What Success Looks Like

What Success Looks Like

Measuring Success

  • More housing options across the continuum
  • Clearer planning expectations and implementation timelines
  • Infrastructure coordinated with responsible growth
  • Stronger partnerships with Simcoe County and other housing partners
  • Community understanding of housing needs and benefits
  • Programs evaluated by measurable outcomes

First 100 Days

Convene an implementation-focused Housing Summit with Simcoe County, senior governments, builders and community organizations to establish responsibilities, timelines and measurable outcomes.

Closing Message

When Jan and I began our married life together, our community gave our family an opportunity. I believe today’s young families deserve that same opportunity. Those who work in Collingwood should have the opportunity to build their future here.

— Norm
