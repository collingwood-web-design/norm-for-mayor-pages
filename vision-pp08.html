PP08

Growing Opportunity

Supporting business, strengthening partnerships and building prosperity.

Why This Matters

A strong economy is not measured only by business investment. It is measured by whether young people can build careers close to home, whether employers can find skilled employees, whether entrepreneurs believe Collingwood is a good place to invest and whether families have confidence in their future. A prosperous economy creates opportunities for everyone.

Growing From Within

Prosperity is built by businesses, employees, entrepreneurs, volunteers, educators and community organizations working together. Municipal government’s role is to help create the conditions in which those partnerships can flourish. The best opportunity for creating tomorrow’s jobs is helping today’s employers continue to succeed.

Guiding Principle: Listen carefully, remove unnecessary barriers, strengthen partnerships and bring the right people together to turn common challenges into shared opportunities.

01

Every Employer Matters

Small businesses, medium-sized enterprises and larger employers each make unique contributions to our economy and quality of life.

02

Respect Private Investment

Council should complement — not unnecessarily compete with — existing private investment and avoid duplicating services already being successfully delivered.

03

Build the Foundations

Reliable infrastructure, attainable housing, transportation and broadband influence whether employers invest and employees choose to live and work here.

Vision for Collingwood — Publication No. 8: Growing Opportunity.

Regional Collaboration

Shared economic opportunity while every Council retains local authority

Regional Collaboration

South Georgian Bay Is One Economy

Employers across South Georgian Bay — from manufacturers and health-care providers to tourism operators, professional firms, family businesses and entrepreneurs — contribute to the strength of our regional economy.

Municipal Government Remains Local

The regional economy extends beyond municipal boundaries, but municipal government remains local. Voluntary collaboration can advance shared interests while every Council retains full authority for its own community.

Leadership Means Listening

Good ideas rarely belong to one person. They emerge when employers, employees, entrepreneurs, educators and community organizations work together toward a common purpose.

Collaboration Principles

  1. Voluntary Municipalities participate where interests are shared.
  2. Locally Accountable Every Council keeps authority over its community.
  3. Not Amalgamation The proposal does not create regional government.
  4. Not an MSC It does not establish a Municipal Services Corporation.
  5. Implementation-Focused Partnership should lead to priorities, timelines and reporting.

Partners in Prosperity

Employees & Educators

Build skills, careers and the workforce needed for long-term prosperity.

Municipalities

Create conditions for investment and collaborate on shared regional interests.

Community Organizations

Primary references include Collingwood’s Economic Development Action Plan, Community Profile, Official Plan and the County of Simcoe Official Plan.

The Plan

Help today’s employers succeed while building tomorrow’s opportunities

The Plan

01

Listen to the Economy

Engage employers, employees, entrepreneurs, educators and community organizations to understand opportunities and barriers.

02

Help Existing Employers Succeed

Focus on the businesses already creating jobs and contributing to Collingwood’s economy and quality of life.

03

Complement Private Investment

Use municipal initiatives to strengthen private and non-profit partnerships rather than duplicate successful services.

04

Strengthen Economic Foundations

Coordinate reliable infrastructure, attainable housing, transportation and broadband with long-term prosperity.

05

Convene a Partnership Summit

Work with South Georgian Bay Mayors, municipalities, employers and regional partners on a professionally facilitated implementation session.

06

Publish an Economic Action Plan

Conclude the Summit with shared priorities, responsibilities, timelines and public reporting.

Decision Test for Economic Initiatives

Opportunity

Will it help people build careers, businesses and a future close to home?

Employer Confidence

Does it improve confidence to invest, expand and create jobs?

Partnership

Does government complement rather than unnecessarily compete with existing investment?

Local Authority

Does every participating Council retain authority over its own community?

Action

Are priorities, responsibilities, timelines and reporting clearly defined?

Connecting the Vision

Reliable infrastructure and attainable housing underpin prosperity. PP08 complements PP01 on water services, PP05 on transportation and PP07 on housing.

Policy Position

Municipal government should create an environment where businesses have confidence to invest, expand and create new opportunities while strengthening voluntary partnerships across South Georgian Bay.

What Success Looks Like

Business confidence, local careers and partnerships that produce measurable action

What Success Looks Like

Measuring Success

  • Existing employers have confidence to invest and expand
  • Young people can build more careers close to home
  • Employers can find and retain skilled employees
  • Entrepreneurs see Collingwood as a good place to invest
  • Regional partners agree on practical shared priorities
  • Action-plan responsibilities, timelines and progress are reported publicly

First 100 Days

Work with fellow Mayors, municipalities, employers and regional partners to explore the South Georgian Bay Partnership Summit, establish a collaborative agenda and define the process for an implementation-focused Economic Action Plan.

Closing Message

Municipal government cannot guarantee economic success. It can, however, create an environment where businesses have the confidence to invest, expand and create new opportunities. Together, we can build an economy that creates opportunity, strengthens our community and gives future generations every reason to choose Collingwood as the place they live, work, invest and call home.

— Norm
